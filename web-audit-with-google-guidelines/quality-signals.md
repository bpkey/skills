# High / Highest Quality Signals (Aspirational)

**When to load:** when the user wants to know "how do we get to the top tier" rather than "what are we doing wrong."

**Source:** SQRG §7.0 *High Quality Pages*, §8.0 *Highest Quality Pages*

## What Google rates

**High** quality pages serve a beneficial purpose and achieve it well. To qualify, they must show *at least one* of:
- High level of MC effort / originality / talent / skill
- Positive reputation of the site or creator for the page's topic
- High level of E-E-A-T for the page's purpose

**Highest** pages do *very* well on at least one of those. They are some of the best content on the topic anywhere online.

## What "High" looks like in a codebase

### News
- Original reporting with named journalists, primary sources cited, real interviews
- Clear masthead, ethics policy, corrections policy
- Awards / press recognition referenced from `/about`

### E-commerce
- Recognizable brand / merchant with established reputation
- Detailed customer service info (returns, shipping, dispute policy)
- Real product photography, original product descriptions, verified customer reviews
- Authoritative for its own products (sells its own line, not drop-shipped generic stock)

### Blog / personal site
- Author has demonstrable expertise *or* first-hand experience for the topic
- Original photography, code samples, data, or perspective
- Consistent voice and editorial standard
- Some independent reputation (other sites link to it, podcast appearances, etc.)

### Reference / encyclopedia / docs
- Comprehensive, accurate, well-cited
- Updated regularly with visible timestamps
- Maintained by a recognized institution or community

### Forums / community / Q&A
- Active participation by many users, with meaningful discussion (not single-sentence answers)
- Some form of reputation system (votes, badges, verified experts)
- Moderation against spam and low-effort posts

### Humor / artistic / personal expression
- Original work demonstrating talent or skill
- Recognized creator or platform
- Consistent quality, not random one-offs

## What "Highest" looks like (the extra bar)

- For news: **original investigative reporting** that reveals information not otherwise known
- For artistic content: work by **uniquely skilled** creators (not just competent)
- For informational: **comprehensive, accurate, expert-consensus-aligned** — the go-to source
- For YMYL: recommendations from professional societies, recognized awards, expert endorsement

## Code-review checks (aspirational)

- [ ] Site has a clear, defensible **claim to expertise / authority** on its topic — articulable in one sentence
- [ ] Editorial process is documented and visible (how content gets researched, written, reviewed, updated, corrected)
- [ ] Author roster is real, credentialed, and discoverable (LinkedIn, ORCID, professional society memberships)
- [ ] External validation is visible: press mentions, awards, citations, Wikipedia entry, podcast appearances, conference talks
- [ ] Original primary research / data / reporting / photography is the differentiator (not aggregation)
- [ ] Update / revision history is visible on long-lived content
- [ ] Topical depth: site covers its niche broadly *and* deeply, not just a thin slice of trending keywords
