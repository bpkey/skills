# Page Purpose

**When to load:** any audit — every page needs a clear beneficial purpose.

**Source:** SQRG §2.2 *Understanding the Purpose of a Webpage*

## What Google rates

Google's raters first ask: *what is this page for?* Pages without a clear beneficial purpose, or with a harmful/deceptive purpose, get the **Lowest** rating regardless of how well they're built.

Common beneficial purposes: share information, share a personal experience, share media, demonstrate a skill, express an opinion, entertain, offer products/services, host Q&A, host downloads, support a cause.

## Code-review checks

- [ ] Every route renders a page with a **single, identifiable purpose** stated in the title and the first visible block
- [ ] Page `<title>` summarizes the MC (not clickbait, not generic, not stuffed with keywords)
- [ ] `<meta name="description">` exists and matches the actual purpose
- [ ] No "doorway" pages — routes that exist only to capture search traffic and redirect/funnel
- [ ] No pages built primarily to host ads or affiliate links with token content wrapped around them
- [ ] Templated pages (programmatic SEO) have enough unique content per page to serve a real purpose — not just a swapped city name or product variant

## Red flags (HIGH severity)

- A page where you cannot articulate in one sentence what it helps the user do
- Multiple competing CTAs / mixed purposes per page (sell + capture lead + upsell + newsletter)
- Title and h1 describe different things
- Generated/templated pages where >80% of content is identical across the set

## Anchor quote

> "The goal of PQ rating is to determine how well a page achieves its purpose." (§2.2)
> "Websites and pages should be created to help people. If that is not the case, a rating of Lowest may be warranted." (§2.2)
